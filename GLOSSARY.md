# Zimbabwe RAC Technician Registry — Glossary of Terms

A plain-language guide to the terms, abbreviations, and refrigerant names used in the registry, the survey, and the [User Guide](USER_GUIDE.md).

---

## Organisations and Programmes

**NOU (National Ozone Unit)**
The Zimbabwean government unit, under the Ministry of Environment, Climate, Tourism and Hospitality Industry, responsible for implementing the Montreal Protocol. NOU runs this registry together with HEVACRAZ.

**HEVACRAZ**
The Heating, Ventilation, Air-Conditioning and Refrigeration Association of Zimbabwe — the national industry association for HVAC-R professionals. Membership is one of the certifications you can record in the survey.

**UNEP (United Nations Environment Programme)**
The UN body Zimbabwe reports to under its Montreal Protocol obligations. Anonymised registry data supports this reporting.

**Montreal Protocol**
An international treaty to phase out substances that damage the ozone layer, including many older refrigerants. Zimbabwe is a signatory, which is why the country tracks its refrigeration workforce and refrigerant use.

**Kigali Amendment**
An amendment to the Montreal Protocol that adds the phase-down of HFCs (powerful greenhouse gases used as refrigerants).

## Industry and Technical Terms

**RAC (Refrigeration and Air-Conditioning)**
The general term for the industry this registry covers — fridges, freezers, cold rooms, air conditioners, and related equipment.

**HVAC-R (Heating, Ventilation, Air-Conditioning and Refrigeration)**
A broader term for the same field, including heating and ventilation work. Used interchangeably with RAC in this app.

**Technician**
A person who installs, services, or repairs RAC/HVAC-R equipment — the main audience of the registry, whether formally qualified or informally trained.

**Retailer / Wholesaler / Distributor / Importer**
Businesses that sell or supply RAC equipment, parts, or refrigerants. They register through the separate [retailer survey](USER_GUIDE.md#7-registering-as-a-retailer--distributor).

**Refrigerant**
The gas or fluid inside cooling equipment that absorbs and releases heat (e.g. R-22, R-134a, R-290).

**GWP (Global Warming Potential)**
A measure of how much a gas warms the climate compared to carbon dioxide (CO₂). The higher the GWP, the worse for the climate.

**Low-GWP refrigerants**
Modern, climate-friendlier refrigerants such as R-32, R-290 (propane), R-600a (isobutane), and R-744 (CO₂). The survey asks about your confidence and access to these.

**ODS (Ozone-Depleting Substances)**
Older refrigerants (such as CFCs and HCFCs like R-22) that damage the ozone layer and are being phased out under the Montreal Protocol.

**HCFC / HFC**
Two families of refrigerants. HCFCs (e.g. R-22) deplete the ozone layer and are being phased out. HFCs (e.g. R-134a, R-410A) don't harm the ozone layer but have high GWP and are being phased down under the Kigali Amendment.

**Recovery equipment**
Machines used to safely capture refrigerant from a system during servicing instead of releasing it into the air. The survey asks how often you use it.

**Cold chain**
The refrigerated transport and storage of perishable goods (food, medicine) — one of the work-focus areas in the survey.

**Energy-efficient equipment**
Cooling equipment designed to use less electricity, such as inverter air conditioners. Step 5 of the survey covers how often you install it.

## Common Refrigerant Names in the Survey

| Code | Common name / notes |
|---|---|
| **R-22** | An HCFC; a traditional refrigerant being phased out (ozone-depleting) |
| **R-134a** | An HFC widely used in domestic fridges and vehicles; high GWP |
| **R-404A** | An HFC blend used in commercial refrigeration; very high GWP |
| **R-410A** | An HFC blend common in air conditioners; high GWP |
| **R-32** | A lower-GWP HFC increasingly used in modern air conditioners; mildly flammable |
| **R-290** | Propane — a natural, very-low-GWP hydrocarbon refrigerant; flammable |
| **R-600a** | Isobutane — a natural refrigerant common in modern domestic fridges; flammable |
| **R-744** | Carbon dioxide (CO₂) used as a natural refrigerant |
| **R-1234yf** | A very-low-GWP synthetic (HFO) refrigerant, mainly in vehicle air conditioning |

## Certifications and Training

**National Trade Test (Class 1–4)**
Zimbabwe's national skilled-trades qualification. Class 1 is the highest level.

**City and Guilds**
An internationally recognised UK-based vocational qualification.

**HC (Hydrocarbon) Certification**
Training certification for safely handling flammable hydrocarbon refrigerants such as R-290 and R-600a.

**HEVACRAZ Membership**
Registered membership of the national industry association; members can enter their membership number in the survey.

**Formal training**
Training received at a recognised institution (college, polytechnic, vocational school), as opposed to learning informally on the job.

## Safety and Work Terms

**PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)**
Safety gear such as gloves, goggles, and overalls. The survey asks about your access to PPE.

**EHS (Environment, Health and Safety)**
Standards and practices for working safely and protecting the environment — for example, not venting refrigerant, using recovery equipment, and wearing PPE.

**Load shedding**
Scheduled power cuts by the electricity utility. The survey asks how often it affects your work.

**Forex (foreign exchange / currency) shortages**
Difficulty obtaining foreign currency (e.g. US dollars) to import parts, equipment, and refrigerants — a common supply obstacle covered in the survey.

**Counterfeit / substandard products**
Fake or poor-quality refrigerants, parts, or equipment sold as genuine — a safety and quality risk the survey asks about.

## Using the App

**Survey**
The 6-step registration questionnaire for technicians (or the 4-step version for retailers).

**Reference number**
The short 8-character code (e.g. `A3F2B1C4`) shown on your confirmation screen after submitting. You need it, together with your phone number, to edit your registration later.

**Draft**
Your partially completed survey, saved automatically on your own device so you can resume later. Drafts are not sent to the registry until you submit.

**Offline mode**
The app's ability to work without an internet connection. Submissions made offline are stored on your phone and sent automatically once you reconnect. An orange **"Working Offline"** banner shows when this is happening.

**Add to Home Screen / installing the app**
Saving the registry to your phone's home screen so it opens like a normal app and works offline. Your browser may prompt you to do this on first visit.

**Consent**
Your explicit agreement, given in the final survey step, for how your data may be used — including whether NOU/HEVACRAZ may contact you and whether you appear in the public directory.

**National Technician Directory (public registry)**
The public listing of registered technicians on the website. Only your name, province, and certification status appear — never your phone number or email — and only if you consented.

**Verification / registration status**
NOU/HEVACRAZ staff review each submission. Your status may change from submitted to verified; you are notified by email if you provided one.

**GPS location**
The optional map pin recording where you mainly work, used for coverage mapping. You can use your phone's location, pin manually, or skip it.

**+263 format (international phone format)**
Zimbabwe's country code. Enter your number without the leading 0 and with +263 in front: `0771234567` → `+263771234567`.
